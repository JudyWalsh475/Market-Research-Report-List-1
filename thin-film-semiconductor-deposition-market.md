<p><p>The thin-film semiconductor deposition market encompasses two primary techniques: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD). CVD involves the chemical reactions of gaseous precursors to form thin films on substrates, offering excellent uniformity and conformity. In contrast, PVD utilizes physical processes, such as evaporation or sputtering, to deposit materials onto a surface. Both methods are crucial in semiconductor manufacturing, enabling the production of high-quality thin films essential for various electronic applications, including transistors and solar cells.</p></p>
