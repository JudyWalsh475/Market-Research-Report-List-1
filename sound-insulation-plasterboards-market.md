<p><p>The sound insulation plasterboard market can be categorized into two primary types: tapered-edged and square-edged plasterboards. Tapered-edged plasterboards feature beveled edges that allow for seamless joints, ideal for achieving a smooth finish and enhancing sound insulation performance. In contrast, square-edged plasterboards have flat edges that create a more pronounced joint appearance, making them suitable for specific applications where a more robust edge is desired. Both types serve to improve acoustic properties in residential and commercial spaces.</p></p>
