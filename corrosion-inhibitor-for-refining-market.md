<p><p>The corrosion inhibitor market for refining includes two main types: organic and inorganic corrosion inhibitors. Organic corrosion inhibitors are primarily composed of carbon-based compounds that effectively prevent metal deterioration by forming protective films on surfaces. They are often used in various refining processes due to their versatility and effectiveness. In contrast, inorganic corrosion inhibitors consist of mineral-based substances, such as phosphates or silicates, and are valued for their robustness and stability in extreme conditions. Both types are essential for maintaining equipment integrity and enhancing operational efficiency.</p></p>
