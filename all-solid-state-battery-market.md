<p><p>The all-solid-state battery market is primarily characterized by two types: polymer-based all-solid-state batteries and all-solid-state batteries with inorganic solid electrolytes. Polymer-based batteries utilize flexible polymer materials as electrolytes, offering advantages like lightweight construction and improved safety. In contrast, inorganic solid electrolyte batteries employ crystalline or glassy materials, which typically exhibit higher conductivity and thermal stability, making them suitable for high-energy applications. Both types aim to enhance battery performance while addressing safety concerns associated with traditional liquid electrolyte systems.</p></p>
