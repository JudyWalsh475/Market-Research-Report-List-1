<p><p>Solid-state batteries for electric vehicles (EVs) are categorized into two main types: polymer-based solid-state batteries and those utilizing inorganic solid electrolytes. Polymer-based solid-state batteries leverage flexible polymers to enhance safety and energy density, making them lighter and more adaptable for EV applications. In contrast, solid-state batteries with inorganic solid electrolytes typically offer higher ionic conductivity and stability, which can lead to improved performance and longevity. Both types aim to address current battery limitations, enhancing EV efficiency and safety.</p></p>
